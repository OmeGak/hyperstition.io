What

A collective oracle that discovers fictions capable of becoming real

Abductive Pathways is an interactive installation built around the logic of the 20-questions game to perform a collective act of speculation. Rather than predicting what will happen, the system helps surface hyperstitions: futures that begin to feel real enough to act on.

The project sits at the intersection of artistic research, participatory systems design, speculative culture, and human–AI collaboration. It is part of a broader exploration into how imagination itself might be designed as a form of civic and cultural infrastructure.

Bring the work into your institution, exhibition, lab, or public programme. Contact us to explore a collaboration.

Why

Social fragmentation and current algorithmic systems can narrow what feels possible instead of expanding the space of futures we can collectively sense. Abductive Pathways explores how technology can help us discover and move toward desirable futures.

Imagination over information

Groups rarely get stuck because of lack of information — they get stuck because they can't yet imagine what is possible together. Abductive Pathways overcomes this by inviting play and curiosity into a safe container, creating space to explore ideas that might feel strange or impossible at first.

Tuuli Utriainen

Discovery as creation

In this system, discovery is an act of creation — like how diffusion models discover an image behind noise and end up creating it. A plot twist on the 20-questions game, but without a predetermined answer. The constellation of questions and answers end up creating the future.

Apolinario Passos
Process

How it works

01
Interaction logic. The system is structured around posing yes/no questions creating bases for concise interpretations to arise through abductive reasoning.
02
Knowledge constellation. Responses are informed by a constellation of cultural, theoretical, and speculative sources. These include philosophy, futures thinking, social theory, art, science, and experimental archives.
03
Emergence layer. The system interprets the patterns and creates a scenario where that future is possible and surfaces concrete nudges toward action, restoring a sense of agency to those who feel inspired.
04
Archive layer. Each interaction contributes to a growing archive of possible futures. Over time, this creates a collective memory of questions and imaginaries gathered in hybrid environments.
Sources

Roots

Abductive Pathways emerges from more than a decade of work in collective imagination facilitation, participatory futures, and transdisciplinary experimentation.

Its development has been informed by: collective imagination processes at CERN IdeaSquare, participatory workshops with the Feytopia community, and experimentation with recent open-source AI models within the ecosystem around Hugging Face.
